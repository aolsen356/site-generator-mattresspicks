What Stomach Sleepers Need in a Mattress

Firm Support is Non-Negotiable

Stomach sleeping puts significant pressure on your midsection. If your hips sink too deep, your lower back arches excessively, causing pain and potential long-term spinal issues. Firm to extra-firm mattresses (6.5-8 out of 10) prevent this by keeping your entire body on a level plane.

Ideal Firmness: Firm (6.5-8)

Most stomach sleepers do best with firmer mattresses than they might initially prefer. Body weight affects the ideal firmness:

  • Under 130 lbs: Medium-firm (6-7) may be sufficient
  • 130-230 lbs: Firm (7-8) provides ideal support
  • Over 230 lbs: Extra-firm (8+) prevents excessive sinking

Why Stomach Sleeping is Challenging

Stomach sleeping is considered the most difficult position for spinal alignment. Even with the right mattress, you may experience neck strain from turning your head. Sleep experts often recommend transitioning to back or side sleeping if you experience chronic pain.

Tips for Stomach Sleepers

Use a Thin or No Pillow

Stomach sleepers should use a very thin pillow or no pillow at all. A thick pillow forces your neck into an unnatural angle, causing strain. Some stomach sleepers place a thin pillow under their pelvis instead to support the lower back.

Consider Transitioning Sleep Positions

If you experience persistent neck or back pain, consider gradually training yourself to sleep on your side or back. These positions are generally easier on the spine. Start by using pillows to prevent rolling onto your stomach.

Mattresses to Avoid

Stomach sleepers should avoid soft memory foam mattresses, pillow-top mattresses, and anything rated below 6 on the firmness scale. These allow too much hip sinking and will likely cause lower back pain over time.
