How We Test & Evaluate Mattresses

Our comprehensive evaluation process helps you understand which mattresses will actually meet your needs.

Our Testing Criteria

Every mattress we recommend is evaluated across multiple factors. Here's what we assess and why each factor matters for your sleep quality.

1. Firmness Level Assessment

We rate firmness on a 1-10 scale, where 1 is extremely soft and 10 is extremely firm. Most mattresses fall between 4 and 8.

Soft (3-4)

Deep sinking and contouring. Best for side sleepers under 130 lbs who want pressure relief.

Medium (5-6)

Balanced feel with moderate contouring. Suits most sleepers, especially combination sleepers.

Medium-Firm (6-7)

The most popular firmness. Good support with some cushioning. Works for back sleepers and heavier individuals.

Firm (7-8)

Minimal sinking with strong support. Ideal for stomach sleepers and those over 230 lbs.

Note: Perceived firmness varies with body weight. A 120 lb person will experience a mattress as firmer than a 200 lb person on the same mattress.

2. Sleep Position Suitability

Different sleep positions create different pressure points and require different levels of support:

Side Sleepers

Need softer surfaces (4-6 firmness) that cushion shoulders and hips while maintaining spinal alignment.

Back Sleepers

Need medium to medium-firm support (5-7) with lumbar reinforcement to prevent lower back sag.

Stomach Sleepers

Need firmer support (6-8) to prevent hip sinking and keep the spine neutral.

Combination Sleepers

Need responsive mattresses that adjust quickly to position changes throughout the night.

3. Pressure Relief

We assess how well each mattress relieves pressure on sensitive areas:

  • Shoulders: Critical for side sleepers who often experience shoulder compression
  • Hips: The heaviest body part that needs adequate cushioning on softer mattresses
  • Lower Back: Needs support to maintain spinal alignment and prevent sagging
  • Joints: Overall pressure distribution for comfort throughout the night

Memory foam and latex typically provide the best pressure relief, while innerspring mattresses may require pillow tops for adequate cushioning.

4. Motion Isolation

Essential for couples who don't want to feel their partner's movements during the night.

Excellent

Dense memory foam absorbs motion almost completely. Partner movement is barely perceptible.

Good

Hybrid mattresses with foam comfort layers reduce motion transfer significantly.

Moderate

Latex and some innerspring mattresses allow some motion transfer.

Poor

Traditional innerspring mattresses with connected coils transfer motion across the surface.

5. Temperature Regulation

We evaluate how well each mattress sleeps in terms of heat retention:

  • Cooling Technologies: Gel-infused foams, phase change materials, copper infusions
  • Airflow Design: Coil layers, ventilated foams, breathable covers
  • Material Composition: Latex and innerspring sleep cooler than traditional memory foam
  • Cover Fabric: Moisture-wicking and breathable cover materials

Hot sleepers should prioritize hybrid mattresses or those with active cooling features.

6. Edge Support

Edge support determines how stable the mattress feels when sitting on the edge or sleeping near the perimeter.

  • Important for: Couples who need full mattress surface, those who sit on the edge to dress, older adults who need stable entry/exit
  • Hybrid mattresses typically offer the best edge support due to reinforced coil perimeters
  • All-foam mattresses generally have weaker edges, though some brands add high-density foam rails

7. Durability & Materials

We analyze construction quality and expected lifespan:

Foam Density

Higher density foams (1.8+ lb/ft³ for memory foam) last longer and resist body impressions.

Coil Quality

Gauge (thickness) and coil count affect durability. Lower gauge = thicker, more durable coils.

Certifications

CertiPUR-US, OEKO-TEX, and GREENGUARD certify material safety and quality standards.

Expected Lifespan

Quality mattresses should last 7-10 years. Budget options may show wear sooner.

8. Value Assessment

We consider what you get for your money:

  • Material Quality: Are the materials appropriate for the price point?
  • Trial Period: Most online mattresses offer 100+ night trials. Less is a red flag.
  • Warranty: 10-year warranties are standard. Lifetime warranties offer extra peace of mind.
  • Shipping & Returns: Free shipping and returns should be expected at most price points.
  • Customer Service: Responsive support and hassle-free return processes matter.

Research Methodology

Our evaluation process combines multiple sources:

  • Manufacturer Specifications: We verify construction details, materials, and certifications directly
  • Customer Reviews: We analyze patterns across thousands of verified customer reviews
  • Industry Standards: We compare against established benchmarks for mattress performance
  • Sleep Science: We apply research on sleep ergonomics and pressure distribution

This comprehensive approach helps us provide recommendations you can trust.
