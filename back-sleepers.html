What Back Sleepers Need in a Mattress

Lumbar Support is Essential

When you sleep on your back, the natural curve of your lower spine (lumbar region) needs support. Without it, your lower back can sink too deep, causing strain and pain. Look for mattresses with reinforced center support or zoned coil systems that provide extra firmness under your midsection.

Ideal Firmness: Medium to Medium-Firm (5-7)

Back sleepers typically need a firmer mattress than side sleepers. A firmness rating of 5-7 out of 10 keeps your hips from sinking while still providing enough give for comfort. Body weight affects ideal firmness:

  • Under 130 lbs: Medium (5-6) provides adequate support
  • 130-230 lbs: Medium-firm (6-7) is ideal
  • Over 230 lbs: Firm (7-8) prevents excessive sinking

Maintaining the Natural Spine Curve

Your spine has three natural curves: cervical (neck), thoracic (upper back), and lumbar (lower back). The right mattress maintains these curves without forcing your spine flat or letting it sag. A mattress that's too soft will cause your hips to sink, flattening your lumbar curve. One that's too firm won't conform to your body at all.

Tips for Back Sleepers

Pillow Height Matters

Back sleepers should use a lower-profile pillow that doesn't push the head too far forward. This maintains proper cervical spine alignment. A pillow that's too thick can cause neck strain.

Consider a Knee Pillow

Placing a small pillow under your knees can help maintain your lower back's natural curve and reduce strain. This is especially helpful if you have existing lower back issues.

Avoid Mattresses That Are Too Soft

Back sleepers are more prone to lower back pain from mattresses that allow excessive hip sinking. If you're unsure, choose medium-firm over medium. You can always add a mattress topper for more cushion.
