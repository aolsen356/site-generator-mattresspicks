What Side Sleepers Need in a Mattress

Pressure Relief is Critical

When you sleep on your side, your body weight concentrates on your shoulders and hips. Without adequate cushioning, these pressure points can cause pain, numbness, and poor circulation. Look for mattresses with at least 2-3 inches of soft foam or memory foam in the comfort layer.

Ideal Firmness: Soft to Medium (4-6)

Side sleepers generally need softer mattresses than back or stomach sleepers. A firmness rating of 4-6 out of 10 allows your shoulders and hips to sink in while still providing core support. However, your body weight matters:

  • Under 130 lbs: Soft (4-5) works best
  • 130-230 lbs: Medium (5-6) provides good balance
  • Over 230 lbs: Medium-firm (6-7) prevents excessive sinking

Spinal Alignment

Your spine should remain in a neutral, straight line when lying on your side. If your mattress is too soft, your hips sink too deep, causing misalignment. If it's too firm, your shoulders don't sink enough, creating a curve. The right mattress keeps your spine level from neck to tailbone.

Buying Guide for Side Sleepers

Memory Foam vs Hybrid

Memory Foam: Better motion isolation and pressure relief. Ideal if you sleep hot with a cooling mattress or want the "hugging" sensation of foam. May feel slow to respond when changing positions.

Hybrid: Better cooling, edge support, and responsiveness. Combines foam comfort with coil support. Ideal if you run hot or want a bouncier feel while maintaining pressure relief.

Red Flags to Avoid

  • Mattresses rated firm (7+) for side sleeping
  • Thin comfort layers under 2 inches
  • Low-density foams (under 1.5 lb/ft³)
  • Trial periods under 90 nights
